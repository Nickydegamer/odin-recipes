Chicken Curry

Chicken curry is a flavorful and aromatic dish made with tender chicken pieces simmered in a spiced tomato-onion gravy, often enriched with yogurt or cream and served with rice or flatbread.

This dish is known for its rich blend of spices, including turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The chicken is typically marinated for added flavor and cooked slowly to absorb the sauce. Regional variations exist across South Asia, each with its unique twist on ingredients and spice levels.

To make chicken curry, the base sauce is built by sautéing onions, garlic, and ginger, then adding tomatoes and spices. The chicken is added and cooked until tender, with yogurt or cream stirred in toward the end for creaminess. It’s a comforting, satisfying meal best enjoyed hot with naan or steamed rice.

Ingredients

For the curry:

  • Chicken thighs or breasts, boneless and skinless – 600g (about 1.3 lbs), cut into chunks
  • Onion, finely chopped – 1 large
  • Garlic cloves, minced – 3
  • Fresh ginger, grated – 1 tablespoon
  • Tomatoes, chopped – 2 medium or 1 cup canned
  • Plain yogurt or heavy cream – ½ cup
  • Cooking oil – 2 tablespoons
  • Salt – to taste
  • Fresh cilantro – for garnish

Spices:

  • Ground turmeric – ½ teaspoon
  • Ground cumin – 1 teaspoon
  • Ground coriander – 1 teaspoon
  • Chili powder – ½ teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Garam masala – 1 teaspoon

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, cooking for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the turmeric, cumin, coriander, chili powder, and garam masala. Cook the spices for 30 seconds.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and form a thick paste.
  5. Add the chicken pieces and stir well to coat with the sauce.
  6. Cover and simmer for 15–20 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  7. Lower the heat and stir in the yogurt or cream, mixing well. Simmer for another 5 minutes without boiling.
  8. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan.
